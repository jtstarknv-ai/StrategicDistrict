What Do Guided Steps Entail?

1

Name Your Strategic Plan

Select your district from our database and we auto-populate your public data. Name your plan, set the timeline, and you're ready to build.

Why This Matters
Starting with real data saves time and grounds your plan in facts from day one.
  • Search and select your district
  • Auto-populate public enrollment and demographics
  • Name and configure your plan
2

Budget & Funding Sources

Understand your district's current financial resources, funding sources, and budget allocation priorities. This foundation enables informed strategic planning aligned with fiscal capacity.

Why This Matters
Strategy without budget reality is fiction. Align strategic priorities with available resources from the start.
  • Map current budget allocation and sources
  • Identify discretionary and flexible funding
  • Align strategic investments with financial capacity
3

District Profile

Understand your district's current state: demographics, academic performance, operational health, and key stakeholders. Build the foundation for strategic thinking.

Why This Matters
Strategy without context is wishful thinking. Great plans are grounded in reality.
  • Review district data and demographics
  • Assess current academic performance
  • Identify key assets and challenges
4

Vision & Mission

Define where your district is headed and why. A compelling vision rallies the entire organization around shared purpose.

Why This Matters
When everyone knows the destination, decisions become clearer and more aligned.
  • Craft your district vision statement
  • Define your mission and purpose
  • Test alignment with stakeholders
5

Core Values

Identify the principles and beliefs that guide decision-making. Values keep everyone accountable to what matters most.

Why This Matters
Values become the cultural compass when strategy gets complicated.
  • Identify core values and beliefs
  • Define what each value means in practice
  • Build shared ownership of values
6

Competencies

Define the key student capabilities your district aims to develop. These competencies frame what success looks like for every learner.

Why This Matters
Competencies align instruction, assessment, and accountability behind shared student outcomes.
  • Select or create student competency frameworks
  • Align to district vision and values
  • Define proficiency indicators
7

Strategic Domains

Identify the major focus areas where your district will concentrate strategic energy. Domains prevent scattered priorities and ensure focus.

Why This Matters
The best strategies are focused strategies. Domains force trade-offs and clarity.
  • Identify strategic priorities and gaps
  • Define 3-5 strategic domains
  • Connect domains to vision and values
8

Strategic Goal Setting

Define specific, measurable goals within each strategic domain. Goals translate vision into concrete targets.

Why This Matters
Measurable goals keep districts accountable and help teams know when they're winning.
  • Set 2-4 year strategic goals
  • Define baseline and target measures
  • Build goals across key domains
9

Goal Forecasting

Project progress pathways and annual targets that get you from baseline to goal. Forecasting builds realistic multi-year roadmaps.

Why This Matters
Year-to-year targets help teams stay on pace and adjust strategies when off track.
  • Project annual progress toward goals
  • Set year-by-year targets
  • Build flexibility for adjustments
10

Central Office Alignment

Map how every central office department supports the district's strategic priorities. Alignment cascades strategy through the organization.

Why This Matters
When central office is aligned, schools and classrooms can focus on execution.
  • Map departmental roles and responsibilities
  • Connect actions to strategic goals
  • Align budgets and resources to priorities
11

Action Initiatives

Define the specific initiatives and strategies that will drive progress toward your goals. Initiatives are the work of the plan.

Why This Matters
Without specific actions, strategy stays abstract. Initiatives make strategy actionable.
  • Identify key initiatives per strategic goal
  • Define scope, timeline, and leadership
  • Build sustainability and scale plans
12

Implementation Calendar

Create a quarterly roadmap that sequences initiatives and activities. Implementation calendars turn strategy into a timeline your teams can execute.

Why This Matters
When work is sequenced and staged thoughtfully, teams can focus and execute.
  • Build quarterly roadmaps
  • Sequence initiatives for impact
  • Track progress and adjust quarterly
13

Stakeholder Review

Present your strategic plan to key stakeholders and board for feedback, validation, and refinement. Stakeholder review ensures buy-in and alignment before final launch.

Why This Matters
Plans that are co-created with stakeholders gain ownership and support for implementation.
  • Share draft plan with leadership team
  • Gather stakeholder feedback and input
  • Refine based on feedback and alignment
14

Review & Export

Generate polished strategic plan documents and presentations for your board, community, and schools. Your plan is now ready to share and execute.

Why This Matters
A professional plan document builds stakeholder confidence and clarity across the district.
  • Review plan with leadership team
  • Generate PDF and web versions
  • Prepare for board and community rollout
